Advisory Board

Stencila's Advisory Board provides us with advice, criticsm and mentoring, focusing in particular on our strategy. Our Advisors act as a sounding board for discussing problems and opportunities. The Board has no fiduciary duites or liabilities. Just like all everyone in Stencila community, all Advisory Board members need to obey our Code of Conduct

Josh Greenberg, Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, New York, USA

Josh is Program Director for Digital Information Technology at the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation. He has a PhD from Cornell University's Department of Science & Technology Studies and was the Director of Digital Strategy and Scholarship at the New York Public Library. Josh brings to the board his broad experience and understanding of the needs of scholarly communication and the ecosystem of digital tools that are transforming it.

Ben Kepes, Diversity Ltd, Waipara, Aotearoa New Zealand

Ben is a business leader, technology evangelist, commentator, adviser and investor at Diversity. He is a globally recognized as an expert in enterprise software and cloud computing strategy. Ben brings to the board his extensive skills and experience in startups, business strategy, and leadership development.

Kristen Ratan, Collaborative Knowledge Foundation, San Francisco, USA

Kristen is co-founder and Executive Director of the Collaborative Knowledge (Coko) Foundation. She has been Publisher at the Public Library of Science (PLoS) and Director of Strategic Development at HighWire Press. Kristen brings to the board her extensive experience in sales and marketing and talents for leadership, strategic planning, stakeholder engagement, and partnership development.

Danielle Robinson, Code for Science and Society Inc., Portland, USA

Danielle is the co-Executive Director of Code for Science and Society. She was a 2016 Mozilla Science Fellow, has a PhD in Neuroscience and a passion for open science. Danielle brings to the board her experience as scientist, technical communicator and community builder and a strong record of leadership, mentorship, and policy development.

Finlay Thompson, Dragonfly Data Science Ltd, Wellington, Aotearoa New Zealand

Finlay is Operations Manager at Dragonfly Data Science. He has a PhD in Mathematics and brings to the board several decades of experience in data analysis, database management and software development and a deep understanding of the challenges that researchers face when analysing data.

Greg Wilson, RStudio, Toronto, Canada

Greg is a Data Scientist and Professional Educator at RStudio. He holds a PhD in Computer Science and he has devoted significant part of his career to helping thousands of researchers around the globe do reproducible research. He co-founded and led for many years Software Carpentry, developing it from a small initiative to a multinational non-profit foundation. Greg brings to the board his experience in community building and software engineering.
